Welcome to Treaty 1

Treaty 1 was signed in 1871 between the Canadian government and the Anishinaabe, Cree, and Ojibwe peoples. The treaty established an agreement on land use, rights, and promises that continue to affect the First Nations communities in Manitoba today.

This website will guide you through the history of Treaty 1, its terms, and how it influences communities today.

Where Was Treaty 1 Negotiated?

The negotiations for Treaty 1 took place in what is now Winnipeg, Manitoba. The area was chosen because it was a central meeting point for different First Nations groups and the Canadian government.

Map of Treaty 1 Territory

This map shows the territory that Treaty 1 covers, including parts of Manitoba. It was the first treaty signed as part of a series of Numbered Treaties in Canada.

Communities Involved in Treaty 1

The First Nations communities involved in Treaty 1 include the Anishinaabe (Saulteaux), Cree, and Ojibwe peoples. These groups have rich cultural histories that are intertwined with the land and its resources.

  • Anishinaabe (Saulteaux) - An Indigenous group located primarily in the southern parts of Manitoba.
  • Cree - Another important nation, with ties to northern Manitoba and beyond.
  • Ojibwe - An Indigenous group from the region of Lake Ontario and westward into Manitoba.
